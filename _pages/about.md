I am a second-year undergraduate in quantitative economics at Singapore Management University. My research is in macroeconomics, with a focus on the empirical measurement of capital depreciation dynamics and their relationship to long-run productivity growth.

My current work documents the steady rise of the replacement burden, δ = CFC/Y, across every major economy since 1950 and the accounting constraint this places on net capital formation. I am also developing a broader theoretical framework, World Ontological Economics, that takes capital maintenance and coordination costs as a primitive rather than a residual.

I plan to apply to PhD programs in economics. My research interests are in empirical macroeconomics, productivity, growth, and the measurement of capital.
